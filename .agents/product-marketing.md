## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Choosing and securing the right loan is opaque — banks push their own products, online comparisons are shallow, and full financial planning costs more than most people's situation justifies.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Going direct to a bank: one product set, no comparison, no advocate.
- Comparison websites: lead-gen machines, no policy knowledge, no human through settlement.
- Financial planners: cost not justified "against what they actually needed" for a loan decision.
**What it costs them:** Paying more than needed for years; failed applications that damage credit files; missed FHB schemes.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of rejection, fear of being sold to, confusion about jargon and trade-offs.

## Objections
Top 3 actually heard (confirmed by Callum, 2026-07-12):
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "My bank already knows me" | Your bank can only offer its own products. We compare it against 60+ lenders — if your bank genuinely is the most suitable option, you'll see that in the comparison. |
| "What does a broker cost?" | Standard residential broking is lender-paid; commissions and any third-party costs are disclosed before you proceed. |
| "Just tell me your best rate" | A rate without a policy check is a guess. We check borrowing power against real lender policy first — no obligation, no credit check from an enquiry. (Never quote a rate without the comparison rate + representative example.) |
| "Will an enquiry hurt my credit score?" | No obligation, and no credit check from a website enquiry. |

**Anti-persona:** Rate-shoppers who want a number over the phone with no fact-find and no intention of proceeding.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current lender's rate has drifted; bank said no; life change (first home, new business, investment).
**Pull:** Local, direct access to the actual broker; full panel comparison; plain-English explanations.
**Habit:** "My bank has all my accounts"; refinancing feels like paperwork pain.
**Anxiety:** Fear of credit-file damage, fear of being pushed into a product, not understanding the process.
