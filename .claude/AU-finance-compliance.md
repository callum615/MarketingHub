## Core regulatory constraints

### 1. Credit assistance, not "advice"
Mortgage brokers provide **credit assistance** under the NCCP Act, not financial advice, unless separately AFSL-licensed. Never use "advisor," "advice," or "recommend the best loan for you" framing in copy — use "credit assistance," "help you find," "compare options."

### 2. Best Interests Duty (BID)
Since 2021, brokers must act in the client's best interests and prioritise their interests over the broker's/aggregator's/lender's. Copy should not imply steering toward a lender based on commission, and should avoid absolute claims like "we always find you the best deal" — frame as "we compare options in your best interests," not a guarantee of optimality.

### 3. Conflicted remuneration ban (2020 reforms)
Volume-based or campaign-based commissions and non-monetary benefits tied to loan volume/value are banned for mortgage brokers. **This directly affects referral/partner programs**: paying real estate agents, accountants, or other referrers a fee tied to loan value or volume is likely conflicted remuneration and prohibited. Any referral arrangement must be flagged for compliance review before being built — do not design volume-based or tiered commission structures for referral partners without an explicit compliance sign-off note in the output.

### 4. Advertising financial/credit products (ASIC RG 234 + National Credit Code)
- **Comparison rate rule**: if an ad states an interest rate, it must also show the comparison rate with a representative example (loan amount and term), per National Credit Code Schedule 1. Flag any draft that states a rate without this.
- **No misleading or deceptive claims** (ACL / ASIC Act s12DA): avoid "guaranteed approval," "guaranteed lowest rate," "instant approval," "risk-free," or any wording implying certainty of loan outcome. Loan approval is always subject to lender criteria — say so.
- **Testimonials**: must be genuine and not cherry-picked in a misleading way; disclose any incentive given for the review/testimonial.

### 5. Outreach consent (Spam Act 2003 + anti-hawking)
- Commercial electronic messages (cold email, SMS, email nurture) require either consent or an existing/inferred business relationship exemption. Cold outreach skills should note the consent basis being relied on, not assume US-style cold email norms apply.
- Anti-hawking-style restrictions apply to unsolicited offers of credit assistance — avoid cold-contact scripts that push a specific credit product without the recipient having initiated contact or given consent.

### 6. Privacy / data use
If using Open Banking / Consumer Data Right data or aggregating client financial data for marketing (lookalikes, retargeting), confirm consent covers marketing use, not just the credit assistance service itself.
